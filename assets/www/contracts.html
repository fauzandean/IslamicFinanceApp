Riba is interest or an undue excess. It is of two types, Riba Al Naseeyah and Riba Al Fadl.

Gharar is danger or hazard caused by unnecessary uncertainty.

There should be Honesty and Fair Trade, trade manipulation and malpractices(hoarding, using black markets, profiteering, cheating, taking advantage of a situation, manipulating weights, concealing defective aspects of merchandise, adulteration, unjust enrichment is strongly discouraged.

All non-observable faults in goods must be disclosed to the buyer, the seller stipulating he is not liable for any effects known as Bai al Bara ah “as is basic”.

Misinterpretations and false declaration should be avoided.

Selling over and above the sale of another is called (Bai alal Bai) and it is forbidden. a person should not enter into transaction while someone else is already in the process of concluding the transaction.

All forms of forbidden (Haram) items should be avoided.

Hoarding is condemned in the Quran along with excessive love for wealth.

Open Market policy is encouraged, all goods should be sold in open market, buyer ,seller must be aware of the market before any transaction is concluded, should not take advantage of ignorance of conditions and prices in the market.

Avoidance of taking advantage of a seller’s helplessness (Bai al mudtar) is also forbidden.

Aqd is the core of legal system, it is a contract transaction between two or more parties for Mutual Benefit and with Mutual Consent, with at least two independent parties, it must consist of an offer and acceptance relating to price and asset, acceptance should match offer, the goods or services must be material effect(transfer of ownership) following exchange of asset and consideration(payment or fee)

There are two types of contracts, Compensatory contracts is selling something to someone else for a price or other compensation.Non-compensatory contracts are something given to someone else without any compensation, (Will or Donation).

Requirements

Contacting parties must be-Mature, adults and minors with parents or guardians, they must be sane and mentally sound at execution of contract

Subject Matter (money, goods, assets, property, land, services, rights or anything else) must be valuable in Shariah and Halal. It should be of existence, the ownership has to by title or as a trustee and agent on behalf of the seller (constructive ownership). The seller should have the ability to deliver. The Offer and Acceptance, where the offer is when a person proposes to buy or sell a good or asset from or to another (offeree) at a price. Acceptance is when the offeree agrees with the price and specification of good or asset. Upon acceptance the parties are entering into a contract.

Conditions

Acceptance should be absolute and unqualified, sale attributed to future date or event would be void. The offer and acceptance should be in the same meeting (Majlisul Aqd) if parties dispersed or an event occurs, then it is revoked, modern means of communications are allowed and if offer is given until 4.00pm and acceptance is given before then it is valid.

Conditions which affect contracts

A sale with condition which is one of the requirements of the contract of sale is lawful and condition is valid. Ex: X will not give keys until Y has paid X the full agreed price

A sale with condition which in support of requirement of the contract is acceptable, buyer gives pledge in connection with payment and gives some form of security (collateral)

A sale with condition which is generally accepted by use and custom in market and is well known condition is acceptable, Ex: A buys fridge from B with condition that B undertakes 2 year service

A sale with a condition which is not for the benefit of one of the contracting parties

Shariah Options of Sale

Buyer’s option to rescind is time limited, the seller has option but ceases before execution of contract, also called the cooling down period

Option of inspection is the right to see or appoint a qualified person to inspect for any form of defect

Option of defect is the right to return if found defective

Option of quality is the right to receive the quality goods as specified

Option of price is the right to fair price in the market

Contracts can be classified to be either Valid (Sahih) of which if it is enforceable (Nafiz) or unenforceable until authorized (Mawqoof). Voidable (Fasid) is a contract sound in essence although a component out of it may be invalid in perspective of Shariah. Invalid (Batil) is where the essence and components neither of which meets the requirements or the conditions.

Form is related to the legal form of transaction and events, and Substance relates to the financial reality of the transaction. Both are important

Unilateral contracts are gratuitous in nature and gifts can be revoked prior to actual transfer, a gift does not imply a transfer in property but could eqaully be related to temporary use or property held in trust. Borrowing and obtaining benefit of tangible asset without consideration (la'ra) is economically similar to free lease where usage the (Usufruct of specific object or asset(car, house) is transferred to recipient for free for a specified period. The benevolent loan (Qard). The will – Wassiyyat – bestows assets. Endowment – Waqf

Wa'd is a promise which refers to an undertaking or a promise by one party to do or not do something in future, promisor is the party making the promise and promisee the party receiving the promise. Promises in commercial dealings are binding subject to one sided, promise must have caused the promisee liabilities, if promise is made, it should happen at appointed time by the exchange of offer and acceptance, a mere promise should not be taken as a concluded sale. Only loss to be paid not opportunity cost. Bilateral promise – Muwaada – consists of two unilateral promises extended by two parties on same asset or service.
