Usufruct

Ijara is a bilateral contract in which the usage (Usufruct) of an asset is transferred to another party for consideration (e.g. money). Ijara means providing goods and services for temporary use against a wage. One sells usage or Usufruct for period of time but the asset remains with the lessor.

Rules of Usufruct

The use of the leased asset must be specified in the contract, the lessor remains the owner of the asset during Ijara contract, the lessor is responsible for the major maintenance and ownership related insurance of the underlying asset during the duration of the lease (in practice the lessor nominates the lessee as his agent for all maintenance issues), the lessor is held liable for any damage incurred accidentally, unless it has been proven to be incurred due to negligence of the lessee, the lessor, in this case could annul the lease contract on application on the application to a court of law, the lessee is held liable for maintaining the asset in good order so that it can continue to be leased, the leased asset should be held in trust in the hands of the lessee, sub leases are permissible with consent of the lessor or if similar usage could be expected, the asset or service must have value.

Certain consumables like money, food or fuel may not be leased, some forms of lease that are permissible include arrangement that are similar to time sharing whereby a specific space is leased at a specific time of the year to a seasonal tenant. In the event of a total loss, the lease is automatically terminated and the lessor has no rental claim from the lessee. The lessors only recourse is to claim any insurance proceeds for which it is eligible.

There is no Shariah objection to structuring a lease agreement with additional security such as guarantees, mortgages, liens on receivable or hypothecation of cash accounts

Types of Lease

There are two types of lease, Ijara as an operating lease, whereby the asset falls back to the lessor at the end of the lease period.

The second lease is Ijara wa iqtina/ Ijara munthahia bittamleek is a finance lease. The lessee provides a promise to purchase the asset at the end of the lease period.
