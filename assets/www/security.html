Security

Security Transfer (Hawala) Contract is the transfer of an amount from one person (X-transferor) to another (Z the transferee) conducted via trusted intermediary or agent who may charge administrative fee but this fee may not be proportionate to the amount transferred

Shariah conditions

Absence of delay (Nasi'a)

Absence of excess (Tafadul)

Guarantee (Kafala) makes the third party liable, alongside the original debtor, either concurrently or sequentially, if this was stipulated by the guarantor in the security contract .Guarantors are able to charge administrative fees as long as it is not proportionate to time or amount of contract.

Collateral Rahn-creditor is when seller prefers to mitigate the risk of default by the debtor/buyer by taking collateral in the form of a mortgage or pledge against an asset belonging to the debtor (registered pledge) or by taking physical possession of the pledged asset itself (possessory pledge). May be sold for this purpose if the debtor does not meet the contractual terms of payment. Any surplus proceeds in excess of the outstanding debt realized from the sale must be returned to the debtor. A pledge of collateral has the advantage that the owner of the asset may continue to benefit from it, providing that he has the creditor's consent, while at the same time using it as collateral to enhance his creditworthiness.
